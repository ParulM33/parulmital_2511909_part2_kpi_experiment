# Recommendation Memo: Onboarding & Activation Experiment

 

## Summary

 

The new onboarding and activation experience (Treatment group) significantly improves paid conversion rate compared to the Control group.

 

Based on the analysis, the experiment results support moving forward with launch, while monitoring key risks.

 

---

 

## Key Findings

 

- Paid conversion rate increased from 3.19% to 7.04%

- Trial start rate and onboarding completion improved

- Users converted faster (reduced days to convert)

- Engagement score increased

 

However, some trade-offs were observed:

 

- Support ticket rate increased significantly

- Refund rate increased slightly

- Revenue per converted user decreased

 

---

 

## Interpretation

 

The Treatment experience is successful in improving user activation and conversion.

 

However, the increase in support tickets suggests that users may be facing confusion or friction during onboarding. Additionally, the decrease in revenue per converted user suggests that some new customers may be lower-value users.

 

---

 

## Recommendation

 

Launch with monitoring

 

The Treatment experience should be rolled out because it shows a strong and statistically significant improvement in conversion.

 

However, the following metrics should be closely monitored after launch:

 

- Support ticket rate 

- Refund rate 

- Revenue per converted user 

- Segment-level performance 

 

A phased rollout is recommended to reduce risk and observe behavior across different user segments.

 

---

 

## Next Steps

 

- Monitor performance weekly after launch 

- Identify segments with high support tickets 

- Improve onboarding experience to reduce friction 

- Track long-term retention and revenue quality 

 

---

 

## Final Decision

 

Proceed with launch, with guardrail monitoring in place, heavily supported by proactive mitigation and continuous validation.
