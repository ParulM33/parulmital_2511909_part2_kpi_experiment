# Overview

In this part of the assignment, I focused on analyzing the cleaned dataset and identifying meaningful business insights. The goal was not just to visualize data, but to interpret patterns and provide useful conclusions that can support decision‑making.

# Approach Taken

I started by reviewing the cleaned dataset created in Part 1 to ensure consistency and accuracy before analysis. After that, I selected key metrics that could highlight performance trends, such as:

Order volume
Revenue distribution
Product/category performance
Time-based trends
These metrics were chosen because they directly relate to business growth and operational efficiency.

# Visualization Process

For visualization, I used Tableau to create an interactive dashboard. The steps I followed were:

Imported the cleaned dataset (cleaned_orders.xlsx)
Connected Tableau to the data source
Created calculated fields where required (e.g., total sales)
Built charts such as bar charts, line charts, and category comparisons
Combined all visuals into a single dashboard for better understanding
The focus was on keeping the visuals simple, clear, and easy to interpret rather than overly complex.

# Key Insights

From the analysis, I observed the following:

Certain product categories consistently generated higher revenue compared to others
Sales trends showed fluctuations over time, indicating possible seasonality
A small number of categories contributed to a large portion of total sales
Some periods showed lower performance, which could require further investigation
These insights can help in prioritizing high-performing areas and improving weaker segments.

# Dashboard Design

The dashboard was designed with the following intent:

Clear separation of different metrics
Easy-to-read labels and legends
Logical flow (overview → details)
This ensures that even someone without technical knowledge can quickly understand the data.

# Challenges Faced

Adjusting data formats while connecting Tableau
Deciding which metrics were most relevant
Keeping the dashboard simple while still informative
I addressed these by revisiting the cleaned data and focusing only on the most impactful visualizations.

# Conclusion

This part helped me understand how raw data can be transformed into meaningful insights. By combining structured analysis with visualization, I was able to present findings in a way that supports business understanding and potential decision-making.

# Purpose

The purpose of this memo is to summarize key findings from the data analysis and provide actionable recommendations based on observed patterns. The insights are derived from the cleaned dataset and visual analysis performed in Part 2.

# Summary of Findings

Based on the analysis, a few clear patterns emerged:

A limited number of product categories contribute significantly to overall revenue
Sales performance varies across time periods, indicating possible seasonality or demand fluctuations
Some categories show consistently lower performance compared to others
Revenue distribution is uneven, suggesting dependency on specific segments
These findings highlight both strengths and areas that need improvement.

# Recommendations

1. Focus on High-Performing Categories

Since a small number of categories generate a large share of revenue, these should be prioritized for:

Increased inventory availability
Targeted promotions and marketing campaigns
Customer retention strategies
This will help maximize returns from already successful segments.

2. Improve Low-Performing Segments

Categories with lower performance should be reviewed in detail. Possible actions include:

Analyzing pricing strategy
Revisiting product positioning
Identifying gaps in demand or supply
If improvement is not feasible, deprioritizing these categories may also be considered.

3. Plan for Demand Variability

The fluctuation in sales trends suggests that demand is not constant. To handle this:

Align inventory planning with peak periods
Prepare for low-demand periods with promotional offers
Monitor trends regularly to adjust strategy proactively
4. Enhance Data Monitoring

Regular tracking of key metrics such as revenue, volume, and category performance should be implemented. This will:

Help detect issues early
Support faster decision-making
Improve overall operational efficiency
# Limitations

The analysis is based on the available dataset and does not include external factors such as market conditions or competitor activity
Some trends may require further validation with additional data
# Conclusion

The analysis shows clear opportunities to improve business performance by focusing on high-impact areas and addressing weaker segments. By applying the above recommendations, the organization can better utilize its data to support informed decision-making and drive growth.
